Profile

I am currently a third-year master's student at the High-Speed Railway Engineering Structure Seismic Research Institute, investigating the seismic response and damage of high-speed railway long-span steel truss arch bridge-track system subjected to near-fault ground motions (hanging wall effect and velocity pulse effect). My research interests include structural non-linear finite element simulation, performance-based earthquake engineering, and seismic analysis. I received my Bachelor in Civil Engineering from Northeast Agricultural University in June 2022 and began my graduate studies at Central South University in September 2022. I am currently supervised by Dr. Liqiang Jiang and Prof. Lizhong Jiang.

New Technical Paper

“Effects of near-fault pulse-like ground motions and pulse parameters on high-speed railway long-span arch bridge-track system” was accepted by ASCE's Journal of Bridge Engineering for publication on October 2024.
